Chief Engineer — Oil Terminals, Caspian Sea

Senior engineering leadership for major oil terminal operations.

Oil & Gas

The Challenge

A JV Oil Company located in the Caspian Sea region sought a Chief Engineer to manage two key terminals. The role required extensive terminal and pipeline experience, health and safety knowledge, and the ability to manage relations with local and international oil corporations.

MSC Headhunting, experienced in oil and gas recruitment, was retained to identify qualified engineers. The challenge involved meeting multiple decision-makers’ needs while maintaining strict confidentiality due to the client’s high profile in the region.

Our Approach

The consultants contacted over 100 suitable prospects globally, including terminal managers and chief engineers from across the oil and gas sector. Despite strong interest, relocation concerns emerged as a significant factor given the remote location.

After thorough screening and interviews, twelve candidates advanced to the next stage. Five were shortlisted for client review, each with detailed reports covering their technical expertise, management experience, and willingness to relocate.

The Result

Two candidates conducted terminal audits on-site — one local Baku engineer and one international expatriate. The local candidate excelled during the assessment, demonstrated strong management rapport with the existing team, and accepted a conditional offer after negotiating a reduced notice period with his current employer.

“I am delighted and enthused by the quality of the service we received from MSC Headhunting. The team understood our particular requirements and delivered what they promised on time.”
Client JV Oil Company, Caspian Sea
