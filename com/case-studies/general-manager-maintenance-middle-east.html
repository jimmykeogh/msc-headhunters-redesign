General Manager Maintenance — Middle East

Senior maintenance leadership for a government-owned petroleum company.

Oil & Gas

The Challenge

A leading government-owned petroleum company in the Middle East lost their General Manager of Maintenance. The role oversaw a large refinery processing over 200,000 barrels daily and terminals with storage capacity exceeding 10 million barrels. Speed was critical for sourcing a qualified replacement.

Our Approach

MSC Headhunting promised delivery of shortlisted candidates within 4–6 weeks. Research teams from the UK, German, and Dutch offices mapped source organisations globally, including oil and gas companies, consultancies, refineries, and terminals.

Over 150 candidates were contacted. Senior maintenance engineers from the Middle East, Europe, and the United States were identified and approached. Each was assessed on ability, experience, track record, cultural fit, and motivation for the role.

The Result

After four weeks, five shortlisted candidates’ CVs and detailed reports were delivered to the client. The client video-interviewed all candidates and invited the preferred candidate for a site visit. An offer was made within one week, and the candidate assumed the role after serving his notice period.

Client Government-Owned Petroleum Company, Middle East
