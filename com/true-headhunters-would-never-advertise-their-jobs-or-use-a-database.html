True headhunters would never advertise their jobs or use a database.

Understanding the fundamental difference between genuine headhunting and database-driven recruitment.

What true headhunting means.

Genuine headhunting involves direct approaches to specific individuals that work for a client's competitors, targeting senior professionals who are not actively seeking new positions. These candidates are content in their current roles, bring valuable market knowledge, and require minimal training since they already understand industry products and dynamics.

A consultative and confidential approach.

True headhunters provide detailed knowledge, experience, and management of both parties' expectations throughout what is an extremely confidential, delicate, and focused process. The relationship between headhunter, client, and candidate requires trust and professionalism at every stage.

This method helps hiring firms gain great perception of the available talent whilst avoiding the long-term and extremely serious problems that mishandled senior searches create. A poorly managed executive appointment can damage a business for years.

The problem with advertising and databases.

Firms that claim headhunting credentials while advertising positions or relying on databases are being lazy and reactive. These practices target candidates who are already seeking change, which contradicts the fundamental principles of headhunting. The most valuable candidates are not browsing job boards — they are excelling in their current positions and need to be approached directly.

A true investment.

Retained headhunting with guaranteed results is a true investment for any organisation looking to hire the best people. While advertising may suffice for routine positions, executive-level appointments demand a fundamentally different approach — one built on research, discretion, and direct engagement with proven industry talent.
