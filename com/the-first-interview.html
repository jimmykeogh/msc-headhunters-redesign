Tips for getting past the first interview.

Practical strategies for securing callbacks and employment offers when job searching.

Look at your interviewing skills.

If you are having a difficult time getting past the first interview then the first place you will want to look for improvement is your actual interviewing skills. It may be that you are not presenting yourself in the most professional manner in the interview. Or you may be saying a few things that show you lack confidence. The point is, if you are finding that you have been to multiple interviews and you cannot seem to get any further than that, then you may want to take a closer look at what you can improve.

For one, take a look at what is on your resume. It may be that your resume only focuses on the responsibilities you have been given over the years, versus the duties you have fulfilled and skills you bring to the table. Expressing what you have accomplished says a lot about what you can get done with the current employer. Also, if you are suffering from interviewing jitters, it does not hurt to do a dress rehearsal of sorts with a friend. This might help boost your confidence for the next interview.

Consider getting more training.

Another problem that you may want to consider that might explain why you may not be able to get past the first interview is the number of qualifications you are bringing to the table. Sometimes you may be trying to apply for highly competitive positions that require a little bit more training to help you match up with your competition.

If this is the case then it might not hurt to watch what trends are popular in your field, then take time to learn whatever you need to learn in order to get on par with those trends. For some, this means taking a class or two to get on track. While for others, it may simply mean meeting up with a mentor who can give great advice.

Find something to do with your time.

A great way to keep yourself busy and boost confidence for your next interview is to get out there and volunteer. By taking on various projects while waiting for your next job to appear, you can learn new skills, enhance old ones, and get yourself used to being in a working environment. If nothing else, you will be able to improve your confidence before going into your next interview.

It can feel discouraging to only ever land a first interview without getting to a second one, or being hired for a job. But do not give up on your goal. By taking on these tips, you should be able to land the job you want in no time.
