The next 5 most important questions you should ask a prospective headhunter – Part Two.

Evaluating a headhunting firm's capabilities, track record, communication processes, and relevant experience.

Building on the fundamentals.

In our previous article we looked at the top 5 questions you should ask an executive headhunter. Those foundational topics covered the headhunting methods offered (retained vs. contingency), the fee structure, guarantees and promises, and headhunting methodologies.

In this second part, we focus on evaluating a headhunting firm's capabilities, track record, communication processes, and relevant experience. These questions will help you determine whether a headhunter is the right partner for your business.

1. Do they understand my business and are they asking the right questions?

The headhunting firm you select is representing your business. They should demonstrate sufficient knowledge to represent your company effectively when recruiting from competitors, while maintaining confidentiality about sensitive matters. Have they asked enough questions to gain valuable insight into your company which they can pass on? Do they understand that certain information is sensitive and confidential?

2. Have they headhunted at this level before?

Past performance and experience should support any promises made by the headhunter. Knowledge of how to target and contact senior level people in your sector is vital. CEOs and VPs require a very different style of approach to sales or technical people, and this experience must be proven. Furthermore, guarantees should be incorporated into legally binding terms and conditions.

3. Can they offer testimonials?

A happy client will be glad to give a reference, and it is vital for your peace of mind to know that other companies have successfully used your headhunter's service. Direct conversations with past clients offer valuable insight into service quality and help you make an informed decision.

4. Assignment updates?

It is vital that you are kept informed at every stage of a search assignment. Good headhunters should send you regular weekly reports keeping you up to speed with their progress against agreed timelines and target lists. This helps you ensure they are working to agreed time scales and also to identify problems or issues before they escalate.

5. Long-term relationships.

When evaluating a headhunting company, it is important to identify if they just want a one-off fee or if they are keen to develop long-term relationships to work with you in the future. Quality headhunters seek ongoing partnerships rather than one-time transactions. Building a good working relationship with a headhunter who understands your business and people and has proved successful can help your organisation flourish.

The cost of getting it wrong.

Poor hiring decisions carry significant costs, making the right headhunting partner critical for business growth. A good headhunter provides market insights and talent identification that can transform your organisation. By asking these questions, you can ensure you are partnering with a firm that will deliver results and add genuine value to your recruitment process.
