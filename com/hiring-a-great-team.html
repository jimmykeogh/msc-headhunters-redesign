Building teams that outperform.

Strategic approaches to assembling exceptional leadership teams.

Perspective

Great teams aren’t assembled by accident.

The most successful organisations share a common trait: they treat talent acquisition as a strategic discipline, not an administrative function. Building a great team requires deliberate planning, rigorous assessment, and the patience to wait for the right person rather than settling for the available one.

The cost of a bad hire at the senior level is staggering — not just financially, but in terms of lost strategic momentum, team morale, and competitive position. Getting it right the first time isn’t a luxury; it’s a business imperative.

Principles

Seven principles for building exceptional teams.

01

Define what great looks like.

Before searching externally, articulate the specific qualities, experience, and cultural attributes your ideal candidate embodies. Be precise, not generic.

02

Hire for trajectory, not just track record.

Past performance matters, but the best hires demonstrate capacity for growth. Look for individuals who have consistently expanded their impact over time.

03

Prioritise cultural contribution.

Move beyond “cultural fit” to “cultural contribution.” The best hires strengthen your culture by bringing complementary perspectives and capabilities.

04

Look beyond the obvious candidates.

The most transformational hires often come from adjacent industries. Cross-sector experience brings fresh perspectives and breaks conventional thinking.

05

Invest in the process.

Thorough assessment processes take time and resource. But the cost of a methodical search is a fraction of the cost of a failed appointment.

06

Move decisively.

When you find the right candidate, act. Top talent has options and doesn’t wait. A streamlined decision-making process shows respect and organisational maturity.

07

Support the transition.

The best hiring decisions can fail without proper onboarding and integration. Invest in the first 100 days to set your new leader up for success.

A great team is an organisation’s most durable competitive advantage. Every other advantage can be replicated — except the quality of the people who drive your strategy forward.
